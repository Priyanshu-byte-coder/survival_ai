# First Aid Survival Guide

## Bleeding
- Apply direct pressure with clean cloth
- Elevate wound above heart if possible
- Use tourniquet only as last resort (mark time applied)
- Clean wound with clean water, apply bandage

## Shock
- Symptoms: pale skin, weak pulse, confusion
- Lay person down, elevate legs
- Keep warm, reassure patient
- Do not give food/water if unconscious

## Burns
- Cool with running water 10+ minutes
- Do not apply ice, butter, or ointments
- Cover loosely with sterile dressing
- Seek medical help for severe burns

## CPR
- Check breathing, call for help
- 30 chest compressions, 2 rescue breaths
- Rate: 100-120 per minute
- Continue until help arrives

## Fractures
- Do not move injured area
- Immobilize with splint
- Apply cold pack for swelling
- Seek medical help

Source: Standard wilderness first aid protocols
